ECLIPSE — CHAPTER 10: "HOME" (FINAL CHAPTER)
POV: DAKIE + SADDIE + LENA. Tone: the quiet after the storm — warmth, civilization returning,
unity, a love finally spoken — ending on a cold cliffhanger. Player controls DAKIE (Saddie playable
in the crossbow training; Saddie is the face of the final standoff).

This is the peaceful chapter with the cliffhanger ending. It must feel hopeful, alive, emotional, and
memorable — then end on dread.

1. REFUGEE KITCHEN — minigame (first glimpse of civilization returning)
   The town is overwhelmed, everyone exhausted/hungry/hurt. Dakie volunteers to help run the
   community kitchen.
   - Top-down kitchen. You serve REFUGEES (not customers). [ch10-kitchen.png]
   - Each person needs something specific:
       Child → Bread.  Sick survivor → Soup.  Elderly → Tea.  Farmer → Bread + Tea.  Injured guard → Soup + Medicine.
   - Stations: Soup Pot, Tea Station, Bread Table, Medicine Box, Serving Counter, Trash, Supply Crates.
   - ORDER SYSTEM: each survivor shows a portrait, a need, and a patience bar. Timer expires → lose morale.
   - RECIPES: Bread = 1 click (fast). Tea = must boil first. Soup = 2-step (fill pot → cook → serve).
       Medicine = limited stock, only for injured survivors; wrong use wastes supplies.
   - RESOURCES (finite — must ration): Bread 15, Tea Leaves 10, Soup 8, Medicine 5.
   - MORALE (replaces score). Start ~70%. Satisfied survivor → morale up. Failed order → morale down.
   - SPECIAL EVENTS: (1) crying child must be served immediately. (2) storm damages power → kitchen darker,
       orders harder to read. (3) sudden refugee arrivals → queue doubles (panic moment).
       (4) Dakie notices Saddie helping → temporary morale boost.
   - ENDINGS: morale 80+ = town celebrates (good). 50–79 = town survives (normal). <50 = food shortage, tension (bad).
   - Style: The Last of Us settlement, dark realistic UI, weathered wood, lantern light. NOT cartoon.

3. WELCOME-NIGHT TAVERN — minigame (serving = clue collecting)
   Player controls DAKIE inside the tavern, top-down. The town celebrates; survivors sit, walk to the
   counter, talk, laugh, argue, reveal rumors. [ch10-tavern.png]
   - Layout: bar counter / kitchen (Tea | Stew | Bread | Ale), 4 guest tables, music corner,
     notice/rumor board, dance/crowd area, exit door.
   - Core loop: walk to customer → press ACT to take order → walk to station → prepare → carry → serve table
     → overhear conversation → collect clue → connect clues on the rumor board → unlock a side quest.
   - Orders: Tea, Ale, Bread, Stew, Water, Medicine tea.
   - OVERHEARING: after serving, the NPC talks ~5s. Press LISTEN to save one clue. Examples:
       Guard: "Saw red lights near the old quarry." Farmer: "My sheep disappeared near the north fence."
       Traveler: "Whitehall had another facility." Nurse: "Some rescued survivors hear a signal at night."
       Mechanic: "The windmill power line was cut, not broken."
   - RUMOR BOARD: collected clues become cards; connect related clues. Correct combination
       (Red Lights + Old Quarry + Whitehall Facility) → unlocks "Investigate the Quarry".
   - SOCIAL CHOICES: calm them down / ask for details / ignore & keep serving → good choices raise TRUST.
   - METERS (no score): Town Morale, Trust, Rumors Found, Orders Served.
   - Warm & alive, but it should hint something dangerous still exists outside the walls.

4. TAVERN GUITAR — rhythm minigame (inside the tavern, at the music corner)
   Walk Dakie to the music corner; press ACT to "Play Guitar". [ch10-guitar-stage.png]
   - First peaceful beat in many chapters: no combat, no infected, no danger. Emotional & rewarding.
   - 4 note lanes (A S D F). Notes fall top→bottom; press the matching key in the hit zone.
   - Scoring: Perfect / Great / Good / Miss. Combo counter; missing breaks combo.
   - MORALE: Perfect performance +15, Good +10, Average +5, Poor +0.
   - NPC reactions while playing: children gather, refugees smile, people clap, Saddie watches quietly,
     Lena laughs with survivors. Combos intensify lantern glow & cheers.
   - THREE SONGS:
       1) "Campfire Tune" — easy, simple, warm.
       2) "Home At Last" — medium, celebration; villagers cheer & dance.
       3) "For Salaar" — HARD, slow emotional melody dedicated to Salaar. The tavern goes silent; Saddie
          watches from the crowd. At the end: no one speaks, then quiet applause. The game's most emotional moment.
   - The player CANNOT lose; poor play just gives lower morale. Audio-synced; songs replaceable via files.

6. VILLAGE DEFENSE HUB — top-down view of the village; get the guns ready & cover the town with security.
   Five minigames (all required):

   6a. WEAPON REPAIR — top-down workbench. Drag parts (Barrel, Bolt, Spring, Magazine, Stock) into the
       correct slots. Wrong placement = weapon jammed; correct = operational. Reward: Village Firepower +1.
       [ch10-armory.png] [ch10-workbench.png] + part/state/feedback art.
   6b. SHOOTING RANGE — Dakie trains villagers. Top-down gallery, moving + pop-up targets, wind affects
       shots, limited ammo. Reward: Guard Accuracy +10%. [ch10-shooting-range.png]
   6c. CROSSBOW TRAINING (SADDIE) — player controls Saddie. Moving zombie dummies; hit Head/Chest/Leg,
       headshots worth more. Reward: Saddie Sniper Skill +1 (sets up the final hill scene). [ch10-crossbow-range.png]
   6d. BUILD THE PERIMETER — village map; physically place defenses (Sandbags, Spikes, Floodlights, Barbed
       Wire) at North Gate / Windmill / Fields / East Road / Forest. Drag-and-drop, not menus. [ch10-village-map.png]
   6e. WALL REPAIR — settlement walls are weak. Quick-reaction hammer timing game; perfect hits = strong
       section, misses = weak section. [ch10-town-wall.png]
